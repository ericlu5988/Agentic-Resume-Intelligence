# Professional Cover Letter Template

**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2026-01-21

---

## Cover Letter Standards (MANDATORY)

### Format Requirements

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| Length | 250-350 words maximum |
| Paragraphs | 4 paragraphs |
| Tone | Professional, confident, genuine |
| File format | `.docx` or `.pdf` |

### Prohibited Elements

- **No emojis or icons**
- **No exclamation marks** (one maximum, only if genuinely appropriate)
- **No generic openers** ("I am writing to apply...")
- **No salary discussion** (unless specifically requested)
- **No desperation language** ("I really need this job")

### Prohibited Language

**AI Clichés to AVOID:**
- "I am excited to apply..."
- "I believe I would be a great fit..."
- "Passionate about..."
- "Proven track record..."
- "I am confident that..."
- "Eager to contribute..."
- "Thrilled at the opportunity..."
- "Dynamic professional..."

**Instead:** Be specific. Reference actual research. Show, don't tell.

## Cover Letter Structure

```
[Your Name]
[City, State]
[Phone] | [Email]

[Date]

[Hiring Manager Name, if known]
[Title, if known]
[Company Name]
[Company Address, if known]

Dear [Mr./Ms. Last Name OR Hiring Team],

[PARAGRAPH 1: HOOK - 2-3 sentences]
Open with something specific about the company or role that caught your attention. Reference actual research (recent news, company initiative, product). Connect it to your interest. State the position you're applying for.

[PARAGRAPH 2: VALUE PROPOSITION - 4-5 sentences]
Your most relevant experience. Use specific examples with results. Connect YOUR background directly to THEIR stated needs from the job posting. One or two concrete achievements that demonstrate fit.

[PARAGRAPH 3: WHY THIS COMPANY - 3-4 sentences]
What specifically attracts you to this company (not just the role). Reference company research: culture, mission, recent achievements, leadership vision. Show you've done homework beyond reading the job posting.

[PARAGRAPH 4: CLOSE - 2-3 sentences]
Thank them for consideration. Express interest in discussing further. Note your availability or next steps. Keep it simple and professional.

Sincerely,

[Your Name]
```

---

## Paragraph 1: Hook Examples

### Weak (Generic)
> "I am writing to apply for the Security Engineer position at TechCorp. I am excited about this opportunity and believe I would be a great fit."

### Strong (Specific)
> "TechCorp's recent expansion into zero-trust architecture, highlighted in your CTO's keynote at RSA Conference, aligns directly with the security transformation work I led at my current organization. I am applying for the Security Engineer role to contribute to this initiative."

---

## Paragraph 2: Value Proposition Examples

### Weak (Vague)
> "I have extensive experience in cybersecurity and have worked on many important projects. I am passionate about security and always deliver results."

### Strong (Specific)
> "At Acme Corp, I designed and implemented the SIEM platform that reduced mean-time-to-detect from 72 hours to under 4 hours. I also led the SOC team through SOC 2 Type II certification, addressing 47 control gaps within 6 months. These experiences directly map to your requirements for detection engineering and compliance expertise."

---

## Paragraph 3: Why This Company Examples

### Weak (Generic)
> "I have always admired your company and would love to work there. Your company has a great reputation in the industry."

### Strong (Specific)
> "Beyond the technical challenges, I'm drawn to TechCorp's commitment to security research and community contribution, evidenced by your open-source threat intelligence platform and annual security scholarship program. Your published engineering blog demonstrates a culture of knowledge sharing that matches how I approach professional growth."

---

## Paragraph 4: Close Examples

### Weak (Desperate/Overconfident)
> "I am confident I am the perfect candidate for this role. Please contact me immediately as I am very interested. I look forward to hearing from you soon!!!"

### Strong (Professional)
> "Thank you for considering my application. I welcome the opportunity to discuss how my experience with security operations and compliance programs can support TechCorp's growth objectives. I am available at your convenience."

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing:

- [ ] 250-350 words (count them)
- [ ] 4 paragraphs maximum
- [ ] No emojis or icons
- [ ] No more than one exclamation mark (preferably zero)
- [ ] No AI clichés
- [ ] Company-specific content (would this work for another company? If yes, revise)
- [ ] Specific achievements mentioned (not just "experience in X")
- [ ] Research references included (news, initiatives, culture)
- [ ] Professional tone throughout
- [ ] Proofread for typos and grammar
- [ ] Hiring manager name used if known
- [ ] Position title matches job posting exactly

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Repeating resume bullet points | Expand on 1-2 achievements with context |
| Generic company praise | Reference specific research findings |
| Focusing on what you want | Focus on what you offer them |
| Too long (over 350 words) | Cut ruthlessly, keep only essential points |
| Opening with "I am writing..." | Open with company-specific hook |
| Closing with multiple exclamation marks | One professional closing statement |

---

## Word Count Guidelines

| Section | Target Words |
|---------|--------------|
| Paragraph 1 (Hook) | 40-60 |
| Paragraph 2 (Value) | 80-120 |
| Paragraph 3 (Why Company) | 60-80 |
| Paragraph 4 (Close) | 30-50 |
| **Total** | **250-350** |

---

**The Notepad Test:** Copy your cover letter into plain text. Read it aloud. Does it sound like a real person wrote it, or does it sound like AI? If the latter, revise to add genuine voice.
